# Structured Abstract Formats by Journal

## JAMA Network Open (≤350 words + Key Points outside count)

**Section order:**
1. Importance
2. Objective
3. Data Sources
4. Study Selection
5. Data Extraction and Synthesis
6. Main Outcomes and Measures
7. Results
8. Conclusions and Relevance

**Key Points (outside word limit):**
- Question:
- Findings:
- Meaning:

**Importance:** 1–2 sentences establishing why this question matters clinically or for public health.
**Objective:** 1 sentence beginning "To [verb]..."
**Data Sources:** Databases searched, dates, supplementary strategies.
**Study Selection:** Study designs, population, outcome, comparator. State who selected and how.
**Data Extraction and Synthesis:** Who extracted (2 independent reviewers). Risk of bias tool. Statistical method (random-effects, REML, HKSJ). Heterogeneity assessment.
**Main Outcomes and Measures:** Define the primary outcome measure including how it was calculated.
**Results:** N studies, N participants. Primary estimate (95% CI, I², PI). Key secondary findings. Sensitivity analysis range.
**Conclusions and Relevance:** 2–3 sentences. What was found. What it means. What should happen next.

---

## The Lancet (≤250 words)

**Section order:** Background / Methods / Findings / Interpretation / Funding

**Background:** 2–3 sentences on context and rationale. End with aim.
**Methods:** Study design, databases/setting, population, outcome, primary analysis method.
**Findings:** Primary result with CI and p-value. Key secondary results.
**Interpretation:** 2 sentences: what the findings mean; what should change.
**Funding:** Funding body and grant numbers.

---

## BMJ (≤400 words)

**Section order:** Objectives / Design / Setting / Participants / Interventions (if applicable) / Main Outcome Measures / Results / Conclusions

**Objectives:** Aims of the study.
**Design:** Study design and dates.
**Setting:** Country/countries, healthcare setting.
**Participants:** Who was included (N), inclusion/exclusion criteria summary.
**Main Outcome Measures:** Primary and key secondary outcomes.
**Results:** Primary result (estimate, CI, p). Secondary results.
**Conclusions:** 2–3 sentences.

---

## Annals of Internal Medicine (≤250 words)

**Section order:** Background / Objective / Design / Setting / Participants / Measurements / Results / Limitation / Conclusion / Primary Funding Source

Note: Limitation is stated in the abstract (1 sentence on primary limitation).

---

## Nature Medicine (unstructured, ≤150 words)

Single paragraph, no section headers. Cover: background (1–2 sentences), methods (1–2 sentences), primary finding with statistics (2–3 sentences), significance (1–2 sentences).

---

## Generic Structured (for journals not listed above)

**Background:** 2 sentences on context and gap.
**Methods:** Design, population, databases/setting, primary outcome, analysis.
**Results:** Primary result + CI. Key secondary results. N studies/participants.
**Conclusions:** 2 sentences.

---

## Word Count Verification

After drafting, always verify word count:
```bash
echo "abstract text here" | wc -w
```
Or paste into a word processor. If over limit, trim from the Results section first (maintain statistics but reduce qualifying phrases), then from Methods.
