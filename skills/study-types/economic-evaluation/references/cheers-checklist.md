# CHEERS 2022 Checklist

Reference: Husereau D, Drummond M, Augustovski F, et al. Consolidated Health
Economic Evaluation Reporting Standards 2022 (CHEERS 2022) statement: updated
reporting guidance for health economic evaluations. *Value Health*.
2022;25(1):3-9.

This checklist contains all 28 items from the CHEERS 2022 reporting standard
for health economic evaluations.

## Summary of Key Changes from CHEERS 2013 to CHEERS 2022

The CHEERS 2022 update introduced or substantially revised the following items
compared with the original CHEERS 2013 statement:

1. **Health economic analysis plan (item 4)** --- New item requiring disclosure
   of a pre-specified analysis plan, analogous to trial protocols.

2. **Characterising heterogeneity (item 18)** --- New item requiring methods
   for exploring heterogeneity (subgroup analyses).

3. **Characterising distributional effects (item 19)** --- New item addressing
   health equity considerations.

4. **Engagement with patients and others (items 21, 25)** --- New items
   requiring description of patient and stakeholder engagement.

5. **Expanded uncertainty reporting (item 20)** --- Greater emphasis on
   comprehensive uncertainty characterisation including value of information
   analysis.

6. **Model transparency** --- Greater emphasis on model validation, structural
   assumptions, and availability of the model for review.

## WTP Threshold Reference Values

For interpreting ICERs, the following WTP thresholds are commonly used:

| Jurisdiction | Organisation | WTP Threshold | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| United Kingdom | NICE | GBP 20,000-30,000/QALY | NICE Manual (PMG36) |
| United States | No official threshold | USD 50,000-150,000/QALY | Neumann et al. 2014 |
| Canada | CADTH | CAD 50,000/QALY (commonly used) | No formal threshold |
| Australia | PBAC | No explicit threshold; approximately AUD 45,000-75,000/QALY | Harris et al. 2008 |
| Netherlands | ZIN | EUR 20,000-80,000/QALY (severity-weighted) | ZIN 2015 |
| WHO-CHOICE | WHO | 1-3x GDP per capita/DALY averted | WHO-CHOICE guidelines |
| South Korea | HIRA | KRW 20-30 million/QALY | HIRA guidelines |

Note: WTP thresholds are not universal and reflect societal preferences,
healthcare budgets, and political decisions in each jurisdiction. Always
use the threshold appropriate to the analytical setting and justify its
selection.

## Usage Notes

When completing this checklist for a manuscript:
- Replace `[ ]` with `[x]` for items that are adequately reported
- Flag items not reported with a justification (e.g., "not applicable:
  single-study analysis, no model used")
- All 28 items should be addressed; items deemed not applicable must be
  explicitly noted as such
- Include this checklist as a supplementary table in the manuscript submission
- If the study is submitted to a journal that endorses CHEERS 2022, the
  completed checklist is typically required at submission
