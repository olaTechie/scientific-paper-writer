# Discussion Paragraph Frameworks

## Main Findings — Opening Templates

### Systematic Review / Meta-Analysis
"This systematic review and meta-analysis of [N] studies — [N] [design] and [N] [design], published between [years] across [countries] — found that [intervention] was associated with [primary outcome] of [point estimate] (95% CI: [lower], [upper]; I² = [X]%; 95% prediction interval, [lower] to [upper]). [Secondary finding 1, with statistics]. [Null result or non-significant trend, phrased proportionately]. [Waning / subgroup result, with appropriate caveat if from single study]."

### RCT
"In this randomised controlled trial of [N] participants followed for a median of [X] [months/years], [intervention] [significantly / did not significantly] reduce the primary outcome of [outcome] compared with [comparator] ([primary estimate]; 95% CI: [lower], [upper]; p = [X]). The absolute risk reduction was [X]% (95% CI: [lower], [upper]), corresponding to a number needed to treat of [N] (95% CI: [lower], [upper]). [Key secondary results]. [Safety results]."

### Cohort / Observational
"In this [cohort / case-control / nested case-control] study of [N] [adults / patients / participants], [exposure] was associated with [outcome] (adjusted [OR/HR/RR]: [estimate]; 95% CI: [lower], [upper]), after adjustment for [key confounders]. [Sensitivity analysis result]. [Subgroup finding with interaction p-value]."

### Machine Learning
"The [model name/type] achieved an AUC-ROC of [X] (95% CI: [lower], [upper]) in the held-out test set, with sensitivity of [X]%, specificity of [X]%, and PPV of [X]% at the pre-specified threshold of [X]. This performance [exceeded / was similar to / did not significantly differ from] the [baseline/comparator model] (AUC difference [X]; p = [X])."

## Literature Comparison — Framing Phrases

### Consistent with prior evidence
"The pooled estimate of [X]% observed in the present review is broadly consistent with prior evidence. [Author] and colleagues, in a [SRMA / cohort study] of [scope], reported [comparable estimate] (95% CI: [lower], [upper])¹..."

### Divergent from prior evidence
"In contrast to [Author] et al., who reported [estimate]¹, the present [study / review] found [different estimate]. This divergence may reflect [methodological difference / population difference / time period / comparator heterogeneity]..."

### Extending prior evidence
"The present review extends the work of [Author] et al.¹ by [methodological contribution — e.g., separating absolute from relative VE / applying three-level meta-regression / restricting to a specific age group]..."

## Strengths — Specific Phrase Templates

### Protocol registration
"This review was conducted against a pre-specified, registered protocol, reducing the risk of post-hoc analytical decisions inflating spurious positive findings."

### Three-level meta-regression
"Waning immunity was assessed using a three-level random-effects meta-regression, which explicitly accounts for the within-study correlation between repeated effectiveness estimates from the same cohort — a methodological improvement over simpler pooling approaches that assume independence between all observations."

### Absolute vs relative VE distinction
"A key methodological contribution of the present review is the explicit separation of absolute vaccine effectiveness (compared with unvaccinated individuals) from relative effectiveness (compared with an alternative vaccine). Conflation of these two estimands, which are not directly comparable, has been a source of inconsistency in prior reviews."

### HKSJ adjustment
"The Hartung–Knapp–Sidik–Jonkman adjustment was applied to all confidence intervals, providing more appropriate interval widths under the small-study conditions (k < 10) prevalent in this literature."

## Limitations — Specific Phrase Templates

### Small k / limited power
"The base-case meta-analysis included only [k] studies, and the waning meta-regression was based on [N] paired estimates from [N] studies, affording limited statistical power. Wide confidence and prediction intervals throughout reflect this imprecision, and null findings — including the non-significant waning slope — should be interpreted as consistent with small effect sizes rather than definitive evidence of absence."

### Temporal heterogeneity in pooled VE
"The pooled absolute VE estimate represents an average across studies with markedly different post-vaccination follow-up periods, ranging from [X] to [X] years. This temporal heterogeneity means the pooled figure does not reflect VE at any single consistent time point after vaccination, and may partly explain residual between-study variation."

### Healthy vaccinee bias (observational studies)
"All [N] observational studies were at moderate to serious risk of bias from unmeasured confounding. Healthy vaccinee bias — whereby individuals who seek vaccination are systematically healthier than those who do not — may have inflated effectiveness estimates to an unknown degree. The direction and magnitude of this bias cannot be quantified from available data."

### Single-study subgroup finding
"Age-stratified effectiveness data were available from a single study, with overlapping age group definitions and no sub-analysis of adults aged ≥65 years; the observed difference of [X] percentage points should be treated as exploratory and hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory evidence of age-related heterogeneity in vaccine effectiveness."

### Geographical restriction
"All included studies were conducted in high-income countries with established childhood immunisation programmes. Generalisability to low- and middle-income country settings — where primary vaccination schedules, circulating strain profiles, and background seroprevalence may differ substantially — cannot be assumed."
